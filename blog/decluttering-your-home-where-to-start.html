Decluttering Your Home: Where to Start

Decluttering your entire home can feel like a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. The key is to start small and work your way up. Here's our guide to getting started:

1. Start with a Low-Emotional Area

Don't start with sentimental items. Instead, begin with a room or area that has less emotional attachment, such as the kitchen or bathroom. This will help you build momentum and confidence.

2. The Four-Box Method

As you go through your items, use four boxes: keep, donate, trash, and relocate. This will help you make quick decisions and keep your space organized as you declutter.

3. Tackle the Pantry

A great place to start in the kitchen is the pantry. Get rid of any expired items and group similar items together. For more tips on pantry organization, check out Organized Home.

4. One In, One Out

Once you've decluttered, adopt the "one in, one out" rule. For every new item you bring into your home, get rid of one old item. This will help you maintain your clutter-free space.

5. Don't Be Afraid to Ask for Help

If you're feeling stuck, don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend, family member, or a professional organizer. Sometimes, a fresh perspective is all you need.
